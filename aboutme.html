Journeys Across Egypt, Lessons Beneath the Stars

I joined the Scouts when I was eight. Each year since then brought a new camp, a new governorate, and new friends. From quiet rural farms to desert trails, every experience shaped how I see the world. I learned to adapt to the rhythms of different cultures, to pitch tents under the stars, and to find comfort in the unfamiliar.

As I grew older, I took on more responsibility—presenting events, leading two camps, and organizing activities that connected people of all ages and backgrounds. Standing in front of a fire circle, guiding others through challenges I once faced myself, I began to understand what leadership really meant: listening, adapting, and creating space for others to shine.

Twelve years later, the friends I made and the memories I carry still define who I am today. The Scouts taught me resilience, teamwork, and curiosity—the same traits that now drive me in research, problem-solving, and every new adventure that lies ahead.

A passionate reader and captivating storyteller

My love for reading began in grade 8, during the long COVID quarantine. Summer felt endless, and I wasn’t used to sitting idle. It all started with one book recommendation—but soon, I was searching for more stories, more voices. By the end of that summer, I had read ten books before entering grade 9, and a new habit was born.

Since then, reading has become a rhythm in my life—one book after another, month after month, until I reached nearly fifty over the past six years. It opened new worlds and connected me with people who shared that passion, especially in my school library and during the Arab Reading Challenge, which I joined for two years.

My reading journey has taken me from history to philosophy, and from Orwell and Dostoevsky to Kafka. But my favorite book remains The Postman of Neruda by Antonio Skármeta. Its simplicity reflects what I believe is the essence of literature, the ability to move hearts through clarity, sincerity, and emotion rather than complexity.

From Childhood Passion to Lifelong Learner

I began playing chess when I was young, but my first official competition came in February 2020, when I was 14. From that moment, chess became more than a hobby, it turned into a journey that took me across many of Egypt’s governorates, competing in tournaments and meeting players from every corner of the country. Over time, I earned gold and silver medals in multiple regional events and was later named the Best Rising Player under 16 in the Egyptian Premier League.

Those years taught me patience, strategy, and resilience. Even at school, chess became part of my daily rhythm—matches with friends between classes or long evenings replaying famous games. Eventually, I founded the school’s first chess club, which continues to this day, hosting monthly tournaments and teaching sessions that bring together students who share the same quiet passion I discovered years ago.
